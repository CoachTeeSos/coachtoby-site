# 🚀 Scripture Reading System (Fresh Start Method)

A curriculum for reading the Bible without inherited assumptions. 7 days, 7 core shifts.

---

## DAY 1: Who Was Afraid When This Was Written?

**Method**: For any passage, ask "Who feared this prophecy?"

### Why this matters:
- Isaiah wrote during Assyrian threat (700s BC) - not about end times
- Jeremiah wrote during Babylonian siege - not about 2025
- John wrote during Domitian persecution (90s AD) - not about robots/AI

### Practice passage:
**Matthew 24** - Disciples ask "WHEN will these things happen?" (Jerusalem destruction)
- Not: "Signs of Jesus' return" (misread by later translators)
- But: "Timing of Jerusalem's fall - only Father knows"

> 🔍 Homework: Take any favorite "end times" verse. Ask: Who feared this?

---

## DAY 2: Hebrew/Greek Definitions Override English

**Method**: Check original word before accepting English meaning

### Key shifts:
- **Kingdom** (βασιλεία) = Reign/rule, not future nation
- **Last days** (ἐσχάτη) = Most intense period, not final chronological
- **Atonement** (כַּפַּר) = Cover/pardon, not penal substitution

### Practice:
**1 Corinthians 15:24-28** - "Putting all enemies under feet"
- **Under feet** (ὑπὸ πόδας) = Defeat/dominion, not physical stomping
- Enemies = Death/devil/Satan - **already defeated** (Colossians 2:15)

> 🔍 Homework: Look up γένεσις (birth/nature) vs συμπέρσβεργησις (perseverance)

---

## DAY 3: DSS Shows Us Oldest Readable Meaning

**Method**: What did Jews read BEFORE the New Testament?

### Examples:
- **Deuteronomy 32:8-9** - "Sons of God" plural (DSS reads: "angels of God")
- **1 Enoch 1:9** - "Elect one" will judge - not future Jesus prediction
- **4 Ezra 2:36** - Righteous suffer, wicked flourish - not dispensational timeline

### Practice:
**Ezekiel 37** - Valley of dry bones
- DSS understood as: Restoration from exile
- Not: Future bodily resurrection (though that's valid too)
- Context: Jews in Babylon wanted hope of return

> 🔍 Homework: Compare LXX Genesis 1:1 (ἀρχή) = beginning/origin, not "created from nothing"

---

## DAY 4: Symbols Point Backward to Jesus, Not Forward

**Method**: Every symbol finds completion in Christ's first coming

### Examples:
- **Passover lamb** = Sacrificed during festival (1 Cor 5:7)
- **Serpent lifted up** = Jesus on cross (John 3:14-15)
- **Suffering servant** = Crucified Messiah (Isaiah 53)

### Practice:
**Revelation symbols**:
- **Lamb slain** = Crucified Jesus (Rev 5:6)
- **Dragon cast down** = Satan defeated through cross (Rev 12:9)
- **New Jerusalem** = Access to God through Christ (Rev 21:22-23)

> 🔍 Homework: Take any Revelation symbol. Ask: When was this fulfilled?

---

## DAY 5: Read Like Persecuted Believers, Not Comfortable Critics

**Method**: What would this mean to someone being killed for belief?

### Shift in perspective:
- Comfort promises = Deliverance from suffering, not removal of suffering
- Victory language = Martyrdom triumph, not political dominance
- Kingdom = Community survival, not institutional control

### Practice:
**Romans 8:35-39** - "Nothing can separate from love"
- To persecuted Roman Christians: Death cannot sever relationship
- Not: Future rapture removes us from trouble
- But: Even under Nero, Christ's love holds

> 🔍 Homework: Read Hebrews 10:32-39 as if you're facing lions

---

## DAY 6: Eternal Life = Knowing God Now (Not Later)

**Method**: Shift from "when we die" to "present reality"

### Key verses:
- **John 17:3** - Eternal life = Knowing you (present tense)
- **1 John 5:20** - We know Son exists with us (already happening)
- **Luke 17:21** - Kingdom within/among you (not coming soon)

### Practice:
**1 Corinthians 15** - "Sleeping" saints wake
- Not: Zombie resurrection at Jesus' return
- But: Martyrs alive with Christ now (Phil 1:23)

> 🔍 Homework: Where does Jesus say "eternal life begins after death"?

---

## DAY 7: Pattern Recognition - How God Always Works

**Method**: Look for recurring divine patterns across all texts

### The pattern:
1. Crisis creates need (Assyrian/Babylonian/Roman threat)
2. Prophet declares hope (return/temple restoration/Christ's victory)
3. Community survives through faith (exile ends/persecution ends)
4. New creation emerges (return from exile/eternal presence through Christ)

### Practice:
**Compare all three**:
- Isaiah 40-55 (Babylon exile)
- Ezekiel 36-37 (Temple restoration)
- Revelation (Final hope)

> 🔍 Homework: Trace this pattern in your favorite Old Testament promise
