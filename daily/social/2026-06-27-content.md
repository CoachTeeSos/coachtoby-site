## C) TWITTER/X THREAD (7 tweets)

**Tweet 1 (Hook):**
You don't quit singing because you're lazy.

You quit because you're relying on motivation instead of systems. Motivation always leaves. Systems don't.

Here are the 3 traps that kill your consistency — and the 5-minute fix:

**Tweet 2:**
Trap #1: All or Nothing.

You set a 30-min daily goal. One day you only have 10 minutes, so you skip entirely — because 10 minutes "doesn't count."

10 minutes of focused practice > 0 minutes of perfect practice. Every single time.

**Tweet 3:**
Trap #2: Progress Comparison.

You listen to a pro singer and think "I'll never sound like that." So you practice less.

That singer has 10,000 hours. You have 14 days. That's not a fair comparison — it's rigged.

Only compare: do you sound better than 2 weeks ago?

**Tweet 4:**
Trap #3: Invisible Progress.

You practice for a week, don't hear a difference, assume it's not working.

But vocal progress happens beneath the surface — like building a foundation before walls go up.

Most people quit right before the breakthrough. Then start over from zero.

**Tweet 5:**
The fix: The 5-Minute Non-Negotiable.

Your daily floor — so easy you can do it on your worst day:
① Breath check — 3 supported breaths (60 sec)
② Lip trill scale — 3 rounds (90 sec)
③ One phrase, one focus — 2 reps (2 min)

Too small to fail.

**Tweet 6:**
Track it visibly:
- Wall calendar + X every day you practice (don't break the chain)
- Record the same 30-sec phrase every Sunday (hear progress)
- One-sentence journal after practice (catch patterns)

Make progress so visible you can't deny it.

**Tweet 7:**
The deepest truth: you stick with singing when you become "the kind of person who sings."

That identity comes from evidence. Every X on the calendar is proof. Every practice is a vote.

Stop starting over. Start building. 🎤
