Your Voice Is Holding You Back

She'd been singing for 7 years. Church choir, open mics, even a few weddings. But every time someone asked her to sing, she'd feel her throat tighten.

Her voice would crack on the high notes. Her breath would run out. And the worst part? Everyone could hear it.

She tried YouTube tutorials. She tried breathing exercises. She even tried a different vocal coach.

Nothing worked.

Until she discovered the one thing nobody was teaching her.

⚠️ If you've been singing for months or years and still can't hit the notes you want — this is for you.

The Lie You've Been Told

Here's what most singing teachers won't tell you:

You don't have a "bad voice."

You have a coordination problem.

Your vocal folds, breath, and resonance aren't working together. That's it. That's the whole problem.

And here's the kicker: No amount of practice will fix it if you're practicing the wrong thing.

This is why most singers plateau. They practice harder. They push more. They strain more.

And they wonder why nothing changes.

The Hidden Mechanism

Your voice has gears. Just like a car.

Chest voice = low gear. Powerful, but can't go fast.

Head voice = high gear. Light, but can't pull heavy loads.

Mix voice = the gear nobody teaches you. Powerful AND high.

Most singers are stuck in first gear. They try to rev higher and higher until the engine screams.

The solution isn't more power. It's shifting gears.

Try This Right Now (2 Minutes)

Step 1: Think of something that makes you sad. A loss. A goodbye. Feel it in your chest.
Step 2: Say "Wah" — like a baby crying. Don't sing it. Just speak it with that sad feeling.
Step 3: Now sing "Wah" on a simple scale: do-re-mi-fa-sol-fa-mi-re-do
Step 4: Notice how the high notes feel different. Lighter. Easier. That's your mix voice.

This is called the "Cry" exercise. It's from the New York Vocal Coaching method. And it works because it bypasses the "trying harder" reflex that's been holding you back.

Why This Matters For You

Here's what happens when you can't shift gears:

  • Your voice cracks in front of others → embarrassment
  • You avoid singing in public → missed opportunities
  • You compare yourself to others → self-doubt
  • You think you're "not talented" → give up

None of those are true. You just haven't been taught the right coordination.
